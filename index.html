Chapter 01 · A tale of two years

When a single drought rewrites a season.

The 2008 drought in the American West was severe enough to depress vegetation health every month of the year compared to 2005, a relatively normal year three years prior. Pick any two years to see how dramatically a single drought can reshape a growing season.

Chapter 02 · The map, year by year

Where the brown crept in.

Each region of the Americas carries its own drought story. Click a region box to zoom in and play an automated story of notable events. Drag the slider to scrub through months from 2000 to 2025. Hover any pixel for the local vegetation proxy, or click Highlight region to define your own area of interest.

        Chapter 03 · The pixels are quieter

        The whole distribution is sliding left.

        It isn't only the worst-hit pixels that have changed. The entire population of tree-cover values has shifted toward lower density. Toggle the years — the gap between 2001 and 2022 is not a tail event. It is the median pixel growing browner.

        Chapter 04 · Every region, every year

        Read left → right. Watch the green drain out.

        The matrix tells you what an average obscures: browning is pervasive but uneven. Sort by total loss to see which regions surrendered the most cover over twenty-three years. Click a row to highlight that region in the chart below.

        Chapter 05 · How wide is the spread?

        Some places drift. Others swing.

        Regional deviation from the long-term baseline isn't only a question of direction — it's a question of volatility. The Midwest's distribution is wide and high, while the Andes are narrow and steady. Adjust the year range to see how the shape changes.

        Chapter 06 · A signal by latitude

        The subtropics took the hardest hit.

        Plotting twenty-year change against latitude pulls a pattern out of the noise: the bands between roughly 30°S and 30°N have lost the most green. Brush a latitude band to isolate it.

        Sources & methods

        Vegetation data: NASA MODIS Terra Monthly NDVI Level 3, via the NASA GIBS WMS endpoint (gibs.earthdata.nasa.gov), layer MODIS_Terra_L3_NDVI_Monthly. Spatial extent: longitude −170° to −30°, latitude −60° to +75°. Temporal extent: monthly composites from January 2000 through April 2025.

        Pre-processing: raw imagery was cached, cropped, and converted to numeric arrays. Annual means were computed pixel-wise, then summarized into the six analysis regions used in this atlas. Tree-cover percentages are derived from MODIS VCF Collection 6 (MOD44B).

        Built with D3.js v7. Final project for DSC 106 · Data Visualization · UC San Diego · Spring 2026. Source on GitHub.
